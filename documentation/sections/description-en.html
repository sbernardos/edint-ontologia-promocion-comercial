The Commercial Promotion Ontology: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the main entities of the ontology and the relationships among them. The ontology represents, among other things, commercial campaigns, which represent a set of marketing operations and strategies that pursue common objectives such as giving visibility to certain products and services, and commercial events, which represent a planned activity to interact with their target audience —customers, partners, or employees— with the aim of promoting products or services. Commercial campaigns may correspond to a campaign type, cover a zone, and be located in a municipality. In addition, they have a number of joined commercial spaces, although it is also possible to indicate the specific affiliated commercial spaces. Other data include a start date, an end date, and a budget.

Commercial events can correspond to an event type cover a zone, and be located in a municipality. Other data include a start date and an end date, but more complex schedules can be represented using event, of which commercial events are a subclass, and schedule from the schema ontology.

For both commercial campaigns and commercial events, it is possible to establish their geolocation through their geometry.

NOTE: To establish/obtain the zone in which a commercial space is located, the property located in zone can be used, but it is also possible to use the location-related properties of the infrastructure elements from the Infrastructure Ontology.

The following shows an example in RDF using RDF Turtle notation. This example describes the commercial campaign CAMP-2025-CH-001, of type Revilatization, and the commercial event EV-2025-CH-001, of type LocalEvent. Both are located in the zone of economic interest ZONA-CHAMBERI-001. The commercial campaign includes the commercial space COM-CH-0001, which obviously will also be located in that zone, and its start and end dates.
